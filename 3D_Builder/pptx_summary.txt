                                  BACKGROUND AND MOTIVATION                            Masonry walls are vulnerable to severe winds as observed during tornadoes at Moore, Oklahoma (1999), Joplin, Missouri (2011), and Tuscaloosa, Alabama (2011)        Even heavy masonry walls that are not adequately connected or reinforced have failed during severe winds (FEMA P-431)       The collapse of the masonry walls can lead to collapse of the entire structure which can result in loss of life       Hence, the Federal Emergency Management  Agency (FEMA) is conducting research to develop an engineering-based methodology of selecting the best available refuge area during a tornado       This study is conducted as a part of the research funded by FEMA       The purpose of this study is to estimate the out-of-plane wind load capacity of masonry walls that can be used for tornado damage analysis of a structural system                       
                                  OBJECTIVES                            Finite element analysis (FEA) is performed to determine the lateral load capacity of unreinforced and reinforced masonry walls         Sensitivity analysis is performed for different parameters       FEA calculated wall capacities are used to estimate the design wind speed of the walls based on ASCE 7       Calculated wind speeds are used to generate fragility  curves for each wall type based on load and resistance concepts       Generalized fragility curves are developed for each wall type and factors are proposed for various parameters                                                      Wall FEM validation                                                      Sensitivity analysis                                                      Wall capacity curves/equations                                                      Design wind speed calculation                                                      Fragility curves for each wall type                                                      Factors for different parameters                                                      Generalized fragility curves for each wall type

                                       FRAGILITY DEVELOPMENT SEQUENCE                                  The structural fragilities are developed in a sequence of calculations           The fragilities are based on limit state failures and incipient collapse           Straight wind fragilities are developed using load and resistance concepts and code-based type calculations (Kennedy &amp;      Ravindra      1984) by using Monte-Carlo simulation           Randomness and uncertainties           Basic Fragilities           Wind pressure zones            Enclosure States           Different Materials           Basic Fragilities are combined to produce final wall fragilities           The developed fragilities can be considered to be straight-wind fragilities           The straight wind fragilities are calibrated to reflect the effects of      tornadic      winds            APC effects           Tornado path characteristics including resulting wind direction distributions            Tornado wind field characteristics

                                  SUMMARY                             Finite element (FE) models of unreinforced and reinforced masonry walls are developed and validated against experimental responses       These experimentally validated FE models are used to study the sensitivity of lateral capacity of these walls for different design parameters        Based on the responses of the parametric study, lateral load capacity curves or analytical equations are generated for unreinforced and reinforced masonry walls       The lateral load capacities of walls are used to develop fragility curves for each wall type based on load and resistance concepts        A reference fragility curve is developed for each wall type and factors are proposed for different parameters       Similar to URM and RM, resistances and fragilities are developed for masonry infill walls       A study is under way to investigate the influence of in-plane lateral loads on the out-of-plane load capacity of masonry walls
